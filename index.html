Monsters and Why I Love Them

Jane Plomp's Music Thesis

I began my music thesis with one guiding word: Monsters. Below are the results.

Note: Some monsters on this list may be more lovable than others.

Siren

Sirens are typically representative of temptation and seduction. I wanted to explore what a sincere love would look like if your lover has a career in destroying people with her voice.

Clone-Me

Sometimes I think it'd be nice to make a clone of myself who will do all my work. This song reminds me why that might be a bad idea.

Mothman

Mothman is kinda hot... right?

The Swarm

This is about bees. It's also about freedom and isolation, and how by making space in life for those who take advantage of you, you can lose your sense of self.

Frankly_Francine

This song is sung from the perspective of Frankenstein's monster, in this adaptation a woman who calls herself "Francine" and dearly loves herself.

Nessie

An instrumental piece. Although storytelling without lyrics is not my usual method, it was a fun challenge for this project.

Nice Guy

Nice Guys are the real monster. Please be forewarned there is some vulgar language and swearing in this song.

Pretty Brain

My neuroscience thesis involved music, so it felt appropriate that my music thesis get a little bit of brain action as well. I do not condone nor recommend the actions or opinions in this song.




Thank you for listening.
